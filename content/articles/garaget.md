# [![Featured Project](https://img.shields.io/badge/Personal-Project-gold?style=for-the-badge&logo=artstation&logoColor=white)](https://www.artstation.com/artwork/2xBxPe)

This project started with a very simple idea: to recreate a garage that wouldn't just store objects, but also small pieces of my own story.

At first glance, it may look like an ordinary garage, filled with random stuff accumulated over the years. However, almost every item in the scene has a personal meaning and helps tell the story of the geeky things that make me who I am.

Among the objects, you'll find references to my hobbies, my studies, and my professional journey. From the Flux Capacitor and the Hoverboard from Back to the Future, to my very first computer sitting on one of the shelves. You'll also spot boxes featuring the logo of my parents' cleaning company (the same company that allowed me to save enough money to pay for my 3D Master's degree. The final installment was apparently paid through some subtle product placement in this project).

The more observant viewers may also notice posters from Pac-Man, the short film I created during my Animation Master's degree, as well as KITT's iconic steering wheel from Knight Rider.

And then there's the true star of the scene: my Alfa Romeo GT.

![GarageT](../static/img/garaget_banner.webp)

I've owned it since 2015 and, although it has spent years trying to prove that Italian engineering is more of a lifestyle than a guarantee of reliability, I refuse to let it go. Between breakdowns and repairs, we've built quite a strong relationship. I try my best to keep it looking brand new, while it does its best to remind me how old it actually is.

In a way, this project is a representation of everything that I enjoy, that inspires me, or that has accompanied me throughout the years. A small personal corner hidden inside a garage full of memories, references, and the occasional part waiting to be repaired.

Who knows? Maybe in a few years I'll have to update this project with many more things.
